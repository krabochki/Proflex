            Programmers

            Who is a programmer?

            Programmers develop algorithms and computer programs. All over the world, programmers are in great demand, their work is well paid. Team leads and technicians (highly qualified specialists) receive more. You can become a programmer at any age. The profession is suitable both for men and women with good analytical skills, logic, knowledge of mathematics (in some areas of development) and necessarily English. Not only practical skills but also ideas are valued in programming. You can learn the profession in universities, courses and colleges, but you can also learn it on your own. Some employers prefer programmers with higher technical education.

            Specializations of programmers

            Developers can be divided into two major categories depending on their specialization.

            1. Application programmers

              They develop application software - games, accounting programs, editors, messengers, etc. Their fields of work include creation of software for ERP and CRM systems like 1C, video and audio surveillance systems, fire-fighting or fire-alarm systems, etc. They also adapt existing programs to the needs of a specific organization or user.

              Here is the list of some application specializations of programmers:

              • Web programmer;
              • 1C programmer;
              • Android developer;
              • Game developer (GameDev);
              • System programmers

              They develop operating systems, robots, work with networks, write interfaces to various distributed databases. They write code for hardware. Systems programmers are among the rarest and highest paid professions. Their task is to develop software systems (services), which control computer systems (which includes the processor, communication and peripheral devices). They also support the operation of the systems they create (device drivers‚ bootloaders, etc.).

            Fall fairs have been a feature of North American life since early in the nineteenth century. At the end of the harvest, people from rural areas have come together to celebrate. Usually, these fairs take the form of a competition regarding the best of all farm products of that year. Depending on the part of the country, and its most important crop, fall fairs can begin as early as August or as late as November. They usually last several days. When the United States and Canada were organized, they were divided into small units called counties. Larger units were called states or provinces. Many of the best-known fairs are county fairs or state fairs. There are also smaller local fairs, and larger ones too, like the Canadian National Exhibition in Toronto, Ontario. Since these fairs are usually annual events, many have developed permanent buildings over the years.
